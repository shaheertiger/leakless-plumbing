Local Knowledge

Plumbing Built Around Scarborough's Housing

Scarborough's plumbing challenges shift depending on which part of the district you're in. Most of the residential core — Agincourt, Wexford, Kennedy Park and the streets around Birchmount and Cliffside — is built on post-war bungalows and 1960s-70s subdivisions, where original galvanized supply lines have corroded from the inside and aging cast iron or clay drain lines are prone to root intrusion and slow blockages. Near Scarborough Town Centre, a growing cluster of condo and rental towers brings a different set of problems: in-suite fixtures, shared risers, and building management to coordinate for access. Along the lake near the Scarborough Bluffs and Guildwood, older character homes have their own quirks, from mixed-era plumbing to drainage complicated by the escarpment.

We work across all of it — bungalows, semis, high-rise suites, and commercial units — and we diagnose before we recommend, so you are not paying to replace something that just needs a proper repair.
