Local Knowledge

Plumbing That Fits Etobicoke's Housing

Etobicoke's plumbing needs shift block by block. Along the Humber Bay shoreline in Mimico, New Toronto and Long Branch, older character homes now sit beside new high-rise condo towers, each with its own quirks — original supply lines in one building, shared stack plumbing and property-management access rules in the next. The Kingsway's established character homes often still run on plumbing that's decades old, while the growing high-density development around Islington-City Centre West and Six Points brings modern fixtures and different code requirements. Further inland toward Rexdale and Humbermede, postwar bungalows and sidesplits commonly have original drain and supply lines that are due for a proper look.

We work on all of it — detached houses, semis, condos and commercial units — and we diagnose before we recommend, so you're not paying to replace a pipe that just needs a proper repair.
