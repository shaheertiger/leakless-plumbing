Know the Warning Signs

Signs Your Sewer Line Needs Repair

A failing sewer line usually gives warning signs long before it fails completely. Catching these early can mean the difference between a targeted repair and an emergency dig.

Recurring Clogs That Come Back

If a drain keeps clogging shortly after it was cleared, the cleaning likely worked — but something in the pipe itself, like a crack, belly, or roots, is still catching debris every time.

Multiple Drains Backing Up at Once

When several fixtures back up together — a toilet flushing causes the shower to bubble, for example — the blockage is almost always in the shared sewer line, not any single fixture.

Sewage Odour in the Yard or Basement

A persistent sewage smell outside near the sewer line, or rising from a basement drain, often means waste is escaping the pipe somewhere along its length rather than reaching the city main.

Soggy or Unusually Green Grass

A patch of lawn that stays wet without rain, or grows noticeably greener and lusher than the rest of the yard, is often being fed by leaking wastewater from a cracked line below it.

Gurgling From Drains and Toilets

Gurgling sounds mean air is trapped behind a blockage or is being pushed back through the plumbing system — a common sign of a partially blocked or damaged sewer line.

Foundation or Floor Cracks Near the Line

A sewer line that leaks or shifts can erode the soil supporting your foundation, sometimes showing up as new cracks in a basement floor or foundation wall near where the line runs.

Choosing the Right Repair

Trenchless vs. Traditional Repair

Trenchless methods — pipe lining and pipe bursting — can often fix a damaged sewer line through a much smaller excavation than a traditional dig, sometimes no more than a small access hole at each end of the run. But trenchless repair is not automatically the right call for every line. It depends on the pipe's condition, its material, and how easily it can be accessed from your property.

That is why we never recommend a repair method before we know what is actually happening underground. Our sewer camera inspection service shows us the exact condition of the pipe so we can tell you honestly whether trenchless repair fits or whether a traditional dig-and-replace is the more reliable path. In some cases, we also use hydro jetting beforehand to clear the line and give us a clean, accurate view before repair work begins.

Sewer Line Repair FAQ

Sewer Line Repair Questions, Answered

Have a sewer line question that is not here? The fastest answer is always a quick call to 437-996-1996.

How do I know if my sewer line needs repair vs. just cleaning?

If a single drain is slow, cleaning is usually enough. But if the same clog keeps coming back after clearing, several drains back up together, you notice sewage odour in the yard or basement, or the lawn above the line is soggy or unusually green, the pipe itself is likely cracked, bellied, or blocked by roots. A camera inspection is the only reliable way to tell the difference. Call 437-996-1996 and we will help you figure out which one you actually need.

What is trenchless sewer line repair and am I a candidate?

Trenchless sewer line repair uses methods like pipe lining or pipe bursting to rehabilitate a damaged sewer line through small access points instead of digging up the entire yard or driveway. Whether it works for your home depends on the pipe's material, its condition, and how easy the line is to access, which is why we always run a camera inspection first. Call 437-996-1996 to find out if trenchless repair is an option for your property.

How much does sewer line repair cost?

It depends on the scope of the damage, how much of the line is affected, whether trenchless repair or a traditional dig applies, and how the pipe is accessed. We do not quote a price over the phone without knowing what is actually happening in your line. Call 437-996-1996, and after a camera inspection we will walk you through the real repair options and give you an honest estimate before any work starts.

Is a damaged sewer line an emergency?

It can be. A slow-developing crack or root intrusion is a repair you can schedule, but sewage actively backing up into your basement or coming up through floor drains needs attention right away. If that is happening at your property now, see our emergency plumbing service and call 437-996-1996 immediately.

How fast can someone assess my sewer line?

In most cases we can get a local GTA plumber out to run a camera inspection quickly, often the same day you call. Exact timing depends on your location and how urgent the symptoms are, so call 437-996-1996, describe what you are seeing, and we will give you a straight answer on when someone can be there.
