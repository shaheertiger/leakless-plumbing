Know When It Is the Right Tool

Signs Hydro Jetting Is the Right Fix

Hydro jetting is not the right call for every clog, but for the situations below it is often the difference between a temporary fix and a lasting one.

Grease-Heavy Commercial Kitchen Lines

Grease clings to pipe walls and hardens over time. Cabling opens a path through it; jetting strips it off the walls, which is why commercial kitchens rely on it.

Recurring Clogs After Cabling

If the same drain clogs again a few weeks after cabling, buildup is likely still coating the pipe walls. Jetting removes what cabling leaves behind.

Thick Sludge or Scale Buildup in Older Cast Iron Pipe

Older cast iron lines can accumulate decades of scale and sludge that narrow the usable diameter of the pipe. Jetting clears it back to bare pipe wall.

Tree Root Intrusion

Roots that have worked into pipe joints trap debris and keep the line clogging. Jetting clears roots and the material they catch.

Preventive Maintenance for Restaurants and Multi-Unit Buildings

Properties with heavy drain use benefit from scheduled jetting to stay ahead of buildup instead of reacting to the next backup.

A Line That Needs to Be Fully Clean First

Diagnosing or repairing a pipe accurately means seeing the actual pipe wall. Jetting clears debris out of the way before a camera inspection or repair.

Know the Difference

Hydro Jetting vs. Cabling

A cable or auger is a fast, effective way to open a blocked drain — it pushes through the clog and pulls back what it can reach, restoring flow quickly. What it does not do is clean the pipe. Grease, scale, and sludge coating the walls are often left behind, which is why a line that was just cabled can clog again within weeks.

Hydro jetting takes a different approach. A high-pressure water stream scours the full interior diameter of the pipe, stripping buildup off the walls instead of just punching through the middle. For grease-heavy commercial lines, recurring residential clogs, and older pipe with heavy scale, that full clean tends to last much longer than cabling alone.

Jetting is not right for every pipe, though. A line that is already cracked, badly corroded, or structurally fragile may not be a safe candidate for high-pressure water — jetting an already-compromised pipe can make things worse, not better. That is why every hydro jetting job starts with a camera inspection to confirm the line can handle it. Learn more about how we check a line first on our sewer camera inspection page, and if the inspection turns up damage that jetting cannot fix, see our sewer line repair service for the next step.

Hydro Jetting Questions, Answered

Have a hydro jetting question that is not here? The fastest answer is always a quick call to 437-996-1996.

What is hydro jetting and how is it different from drain snaking?

Drain snaking (cabling) pushes a rotating cable through a clog to punch an opening and pull out what it can reach, but it often leaves grease, scale, and debris stuck to the pipe walls. Hydro jetting uses a high-pressure water stream to scour the full interior diameter of the pipe clean, removing buildup from the walls rather than just opening a path through the middle. Call 437-996-1996 to find out which approach fits your line.

Is hydro jetting safe for old pipes?

It depends on the pipe's age, material, and condition. Hydro jetting is safe for most pipe types, but a line that is already cracked, severely corroded, or badly deteriorated may not be a good candidate for high-pressure jetting. That is why we run a camera inspection first to check the condition of the pipe before we jet it — we would rather tell you jetting is not the right move than risk damaging a fragile line. Call 437-996-1996 and we will walk you through it.

Do you offer hydro jetting for restaurants and commercial kitchens?

Yes. Grease-heavy commercial kitchen lines are one of the best uses for hydro jetting — cabling alone rarely clears grease-coated pipe walls for long, while jetting scours the line clean. We work with restaurants and commercial kitchens across the GTA on both emergency grease line jetting and scheduled preventive maintenance. Call 437-996-1996 for commercial service.

How often should a property get preventive hydro jetting?

It varies by property. Restaurants and commercial kitchens with heavy grease loads often benefit from jetting on a regular schedule, while a multi-unit building or a home with a history of recurring clogs may need it less often. Call 437-996-1996 and we can recommend a schedule based on your line's history.

How fast can you schedule hydro jetting?

In most cases we can get a plumber out quickly, often within the same day depending on your location and the day's schedule. Because hydro jetting starts with a camera inspection, we will also give you an honest read on timing once we see the condition of the line. Call 437-996-1996 and we will tell you straight away what is realistic.
