FAQ

Sewer Backup & Line Repair Questions

Quick answers. When in doubt, call 437-996-1996.

What should I do if sewage is backing up into my basement?

Keep everyone away from the affected area — sewage backup is a health hazard. Avoid running water anywhere in the house until the blockage is cleared. Call 437-996-1996 right away for emergency sewer backup response.

Do you do sewer camera inspections?

Yes. A sewer camera inspection lets us see exactly what's happening inside your sewer line — roots, cracks, blockages, or bellies — before recommending a repair. Call 437-996-1996 to book one.

What is a backwater valve and do I need one?

A backwater valve prevents municipal sewer backups from entering your home during heavy rain or system surcharges. If you've had a backup before, or live in an older area with combined sewers, call 437-996-1996 to see if installing one makes sense for your property.

Why does my basement smell like sewage even without a backup?

A sewer smell without visible backup often points to a dry floor drain trap, a cracked vent pipe, or a small leak in the sewer line. Call 437-996-1996 and we'll track down the source.
