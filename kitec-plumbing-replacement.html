Kitec Plumbing Questions

Quick answers. When in doubt, call 437-996-1996.

What is Kitec plumbing and why does it need to be replaced?

Kitec is a brand of plumbing pipe with aluminum-core fittings, installed in many GTA homes roughly between 1995 and 2007, that is prone to corrosion and fitting failure over time. It was subject to a class-action settlement and many insurers now flag it as a risk. Call 437-996-1996 if you suspect Kitec plumbing in your home.

How do I know if my home has Kitec plumbing?

Kitec pipe is typically orange (hot) or blue/grey (cold) with brass or aluminum fittings, often stamped "Kitec" or "PlumbBetter" along the pipe. If your home was built or renovated between the mid-1990s and mid-2000s, call 437-996-1996 and we can inspect and confirm.

Will my home insurance require Kitec plumbing replacement?

Some insurers in Ontario now require Kitec plumbing to be replaced, or charge higher premiums or deny claims related to it. If your insurer has flagged Kitec plumbing in your home, call 437-996-1996 and we'll provide an assessment and replacement quote you can share with them.

How much does Kitec plumbing replacement cost?

Cost depends on the size of your home and how accessible the existing pipe is. Call 437-996-1996 and describe your situation — including any insurance requirement — and we'll give you honest, upfront pricing over the phone.
